Under the Hood

Unbound uses the zapret2 (winws2) engine by bol-van, which manipulates TCP packets at the DPI level using techniques like TCP desynchronization and packet reordering (Multidisorder). Unlike VPNs, your traffic never leaves your direct connection — only blocked domains get modified packets.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Unbound and how does it work?

Unbound is a free, open-source DPI (Deep Packet Inspection) bypass tool for Russia. It uses the zapret2 engine to manipulate TCP packets — specifically using techniques like TCP desynchronization and packet reordering (Multidisorder) — so that DPI systems like TSPU cannot properly analyze and block your traffic to sites like YouTube, Discord, Twitter, and Instagram.

How is this different from a VPN?

VPNs route all your traffic through a foreign server, which slows down your connection. Unbound only modifies packets to blocked domains — your bank, government sites, and unblocked services work normally. Zero speed loss for your regular browsing.

Why does it need Administrator rights?

Unbound uses the WinDivert driver to intercept and modify network packets at the kernel level. This requires Administrator privileges. The program registers itself with Windows Task Scheduler for silent auto-start without repeated UAC prompts.

What if Auto-Tune doesn't find a working profile?

Auto-Tune tests 70+ profiles against multiple targets simultaneously. In rare cases, your ISP may require a specific combination. Try manual profile selection, check the "Dev Diary" log for details, and report your results on GitHub Issues.

Is this legal?

Unbound is an open-source tool (GPL-3.0) that modifies only your own outbound packets. It does not hack, spoof, or intercept other people's traffic. Whether using it violates local laws depends on your jurisdiction — consult local regulations.

What is "Discord Hygiene"?

A built-in feature that automatically cleans the Discord client cache on startup. This prevents session poisoning and resolves the endless "Connecting..." issue that often plagues Discord users in blocked regions.
